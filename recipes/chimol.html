Chimol - Salvadorian Dish

Description

Chimol is the "radish" version of Pico de Gallo, A type of salsa commonly used in Mexican cuisine. Traditionally made from chopped tomato, onion, serrano peppers, salt, lime juice and cilantro. Tomatoes release a good amount of moisture, so you will see juice bottom of the bowl so best serve it with a spoon or large serving fork.

With Chimol, Radish is added to give that peppery, nose-clearing spice while also adding some spice or "chile" to get your tongue tingling. Can be eaten as a salad or more commonly as a side to complement steak with rice!

Ingredients

  • 2 radishes, finely chopped
  • 1 bunch cilantro(more than 5), chopped
  • 3 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 pinches salt or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (Optional) add your own spice if you want!
  • 1 lemon, juiced

Steps

  • Use ripe red tomatoes
  • Chop your ingredients very finely: tomato,onion,spices,cilantro, etc...
  • Add salt & lemon juice to your taste
  • Stir until the flavors blend
  • Serve with a slotted spoon or large serving fork.
